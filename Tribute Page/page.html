Early Life

Virat Kohli was born on November 5, 1988 in Delhi, India. His father, Prem Kohli, was a criminal lawyer and his mother, Saroj Kohli, is a housewife. He has an older brother, Vikash, and an older sister, Bhavna.

Virat Kohli was interested in cricket from a young age and started playing for the Delhi Under-15 team when he was just 14 years old. He went on to play for the Delhi Under-17 and Under-19 teams, and was eventually selected for the Indian team.

Career

Virat Kohli made his international debut for India in August 2008 in an ODI match against Sri Lanka. He made his Test debut in June 2011 against the West Indies. Since then, he has established himself as one of the best batsmen in the world and has broken numerous records.

Virat Kohli was appointed as the captain of the Indian cricket team in all formats in January 2017, following the resignation of MS Dhoni. Under his captaincy, the Indian team has achieved numerous victories and has been ranked number one in the world in both Tests and ODIs.

Awards and Achievements

  • ICC Cricketer of the Year - 2017, 2018
  • Wisden Leading Cricketer in the World - 2016, 2017, 2018
  • Padma Shri - India's fourth highest civilian award - 2017
  • Arjuna Award - India's second highest sporting award - 2013
  • Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy for ICC Cricketer of the Year - 2017, 2018
  • ICC ODI Player of the Year - 2012, 2017
  • ICC Test Player of the Year - 2018

Personal Life

Virat Kohli married Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma in December 2017 in a private ceremony in Italy. The couple had been dating for several years before getting married.

Virat Kohli is known for his discipline and dedication to his fitness. He follows a strict diet and fitness regimen and is often praised for his athleticism on the cricket field.

Charity Work

Virat Kohli is actively involved in several charitable causes. He started the Virat Kohli Foundation in 2013 to help underprivileged children and support young athletes in India.

He is also a supporter of animal welfare and has worked with PETA India to promote the adoption of homeless animals and raise awareness about animal cruelty.

Final Thoughts

Virat Kohli is not only a great cricketer, but also a great role model. His dedication, discipline, and passion for the sport have inspired millions of people around the world.

As he continues to break records and lead the Indian cricket team to victory, we can only imagine what else he will achieve in the years to come.
